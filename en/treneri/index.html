Jevgēņijs Minajevs

Black belt, 2. degree

(Helio Gracie > Pedro Sauer > Greg Nelson > Jevgēņijs Minajevs)

He has been involved in freestyle wrestling since childhood, but in 2001, while living in Minnesota, he began his Brazilian Jiu Jitsu journey under the guidance of excellent coach Greg Nelson. While training in Minnesota, he learned MMA-oriented grappling, classic Gracie Jiu Jitsu, Shooto, Catch Wrestling and Combat Submission Wrestling styles. In 2012, he became the first Latvian to obtain a black belt in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu. Returning to Latvia, he opened his own wrestling club, where to this day he continues to transfer knowledge to Latvian athletes and wrestling enthusiasts. Extensive experience in competitions: professional MMA fights, multiple Latvian champion in freestyle wrestling, as well as various Brazilian jiu jitsu and grappling tournaments.

Coaching: Basics, Advanced, KIDS 10–15, Private classes

Sergejs Vartanovs

He began his journey in the world of jiu-jitsu in October 2014 under the guidance of Evgeny Minaev, the first black belt in Latvia, who is a graduate of Greg Nelson. In 2017, he began to focus on the study of no-gi jiu-jitsu. The basis of stylistics is the study of the "ashi garami" system, the implementation of which directly depends on the interaction with the "guard" system. This type of game made it possible to win victories in the Baltic tournaments according to the ADCC rules. In 2020, in Riga, a series of "Leg locks workshop" was implemented, aimed at familiarizing local adherents of jiu-jitsu / grappling with the ashi garami system. Also on this topic, in cooperation with Finnish colleagues (SC "Achilles"), this series was launched in Finland.

Coaches: No gi/leglocks, Private classes
